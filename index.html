Switched Capacitor Biquad Filter

Timeline: Sep 2025 – Dec 2025
Technology: 50 nm CMOS
Tools: Cadence Virtuoso, Spectre, LTSpice, MATLAB
Supply Voltage: 1.0 V

Project Objective

Designed a fully differential switched-capacitor (SC) biquadratic low-pass filter targeted for WiFi receiver front-end applications. The goal was to implement a second-order low-Q elliptic response operating at 10 MHz sampling frequency, while maintaining stability across PVT corners.

Two-Stage OTA Design

The core building block of the SC filter was a fully differential two-stage OTA with Miller compensation and Common Mode Feedback (CMFB).

  • Topology: Differential Pair + Common Source Second Stage
  • Compensation: Miller RC (R ≈ 1/gm)
  • Design Methodology: gm/Id based sizing
  • CMFB: Resistive sensing with active feedback loop

Measured OTA Performance (Fully Differential)

  • DC Gain: 38.5 dB
  • Unity Gain Frequency: 532 MHz
  • Phase Margin: 62°
  • Bandwidth: 17 MHz
  • Slew Rate: 27.3 V/µs
  • Input-Referred Noise: 16.8 nV/√Hz

Although the gain target of 60 dB was not fully met, the OTA demonstrated strong bandwidth and acceptable phase margin for switched-capacitor operation.

PVT Analysis

The OTA was validated across TT, SS, and FF corners at temperatures of 0°C, 30°C, and 70°C.

  • Gain degradation observed at SS corner (down to ~24 dB)
  • Phase Margin varied from 40° (TT) to 80° (FF)
  • UGF ranged from 377 MHz (SS) to 550 MHz (FF)

Corner analysis revealed gain sensitivity to process variations, which directly impacted the linearity of the final switched-capacitor implementation.

Single-Ended SC Implementation

The first implementation was a single-ended low-Q switched-capacitor biquad. Capacitor ratios were calculated directly from the derived transfer function.

  • Reconstruction filter: 2nd order Sallen-Key
  • FFT analysis performed for linearity validation
  • Observed distortion attributed to limited OTA gain

The design successfully demonstrated frequency selectivity, though harmonic distortion indicated insufficient loop gain. The transfer function was first derived in MATLAB (z-domain), then mapped into an equivalent switched-capacitor implementation. The low-Q architecture was selected to ensure stability under finite OTA gain and limited phase margin conditions.

Fully Differential SC Biquad

The architecture was extended to a fully differential configuration by mirroring and phase-flipping the capacitor network. While theoretically sound, the design exhibited instability due to:

  • Finite OTA gain
  • Reduced phase margin under load
  • CMFB interaction with switching transients

This highlighted the critical dependence of SC filters on high-gain, high-phase-margin OTAs.

Key Learnings

  • Practical limits of gm/Id design in scaled CMOS
  • Impact of finite gain on discrete-time filter accuracy
  • Tradeoffs between swing, gain, and bandwidth in two-stage OTAs
  • Importance of robust CMFB in fully differential SC systems
  • Corner-driven instability mechanisms in mixed-signal blocks

This project significantly deepened my understanding of analog IC design tradeoffs and switched-capacitor filter implementation in nanoscale CMOS technologies.

Image Comparator Circuit

Timeline: Apr 2025 – May 2025
Tools: Cadence Virtuoso, HSPICE, Assura, CosmosScope

Developed an image comparator circuit to identify differences in binary-encoded grayscale images, using XOR gates and adders for pixel-level comparison on Cadence Virtuoso and simulated in HSPICE.

  • Parallel-In Parallel-Out (PIPO) register for efficient data handling.
  • Parallel-In Serial-Out (PISO) register for serialized output.
  • On-chip clock divider to operate at 1/4 of the 2.4 GHz clock.
  • DRC and LVS checks in Assura, simulation with CosmosScope.
  • Off-chip driver debugged to drive output from 1.1V to 1.8V at 3 GHz with 12 pF load.

Low Dropout Regulator

Timeline: Apr 2025 – May 2025
Tools: Cadence Virtuoso, Analog IC Design

Designed a low dropout regulator (LDO) to meet stringent specifications under load and line regulations, varying load currents, and phase margin requirements.

  • Folded Cascode OTA: High gain, low-voltage error amplifier with superior input/output range.
  • PMOS Pass Transistor: Enabled low dropout voltage operation under tight headroom.
  • Voltage Scalability: Achieved regulated outputs of 1.0V, 1.2V, and 1.4V via reference scaling.
